The Project

What is this Model?

This project is a functional, scaled model demonstrating a multi-stage process for waste water treatment. It's designed to be eco-friendly, efficient, and adaptable for various scales, from a small community to industrial applications.

The core idea is to not just "dispose" of water, but to "renew" it. By simulating natural filtration processes combined with modern technology, this model proves that sustainable water management is within our reach.

How It Works

A Simple 3-Stage Process

1. Primary Filtration

Removes large solids and debris through physical screening and sedimentation tanks. This initial step prepares the water for finer, more complex treatment.

2. Biological Treatment

Uses beneficial microorganisms to break down dissolved organic matter. This natural process effectively "digests" pollutants, cleaning the water.

3. Tertiary & Reuse

The final stage involves disinfection (e.g., UV light) and advanced filtration, producing clean, safe water ready for irrigation, industrial use, or return to the environment.
